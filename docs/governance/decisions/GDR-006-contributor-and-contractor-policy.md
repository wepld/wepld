# GDR-006 — Contributor and Contractor Policy

- **Status:** Adopted (founder decision, 2026-07-19)
- **Prerequisites:** GDR-001, GDR-002, GDR-003

## Context

WePLD has exactly one authorized participant: the founder. Inbound
contributions without executed agreements would create ownership
ambiguity, and contractor or employee work product does not
automatically belong to the hiring party in every jurisdiction.

## Decision

WePLD is closed to unsolicited external contributions (root
`CONTRIBUTING.md`). Pull requests from unauthorized external parties
are not accepted; repository access does not authorize contribution;
no material may be submitted without prior written authorization.
Before any future contributor, contractor, or employee begins work,
counsel-reviewed written instruments must be executed covering:
present-and-future IP assignment under the jurisdictionally correct
mechanism; confidentiality; originality representations and third-party
disclosure; patent cooperation; moral-rights handling to the extent the
jurisdiction permits; and termination obligations. No CLA or agreement
text is drafted by this record — drafting is expressly deferred to
qualified counsel.

## Rationale

A closed posture with a hard instrument gate is the only position that
creates no inbound-rights ambiguity while the project has no counsel-
reviewed agreements. Improvised templates would be worse than none.

## Consequences

External pull requests are closed unreviewed as to content; onboarding
anyone becomes a two-step event (counsel instruments, then access per
`../REPOSITORY_ACCESS_CONTROL.md`); AI agents remain tools under
GDR-004, not contributors.

## Legal-review boundary

Every instrument contemplated here is a mandatory legal-review item in
the relevant jurisdiction(s) **before use** — none may be adopted from
this record alone.

## Supersession rules

Opening contributions, or adopting any participation instrument, requires
a successor GDR linking here plus the counsel-reviewed documents.
