# 29 — Future Vision

## Long-horizon aspiration

WePLD evolves from a trusted local engineering studio into a composable operating system for engineering organizations. Its advantage should compound: every accepted mission yields governed evidence, reusable skills, validated patterns, and decision history that makes the next mission more reliable—not simply a larger prompt history.

The near-term product promise is **Different brains. Same engineering truth.** This means contract-equivalent accepted outcomes under one approved specification, policy, architecture, quality, security, and evidence bar. It does not mean byte-identical code or equal model capability, cost, speed, retries, context needs, or escalation frequency.

## Vision horizons

These are labelled **VH1–VH5** to avoid collision with the delivery milestones H1–H9.

| Vision horizon | Capability | Architectural prerequisite |
| --- | --- | --- |
| VH1 — Trusted local studio | one user, one project, native governed specification-to-acceptance workflow | Build Feature Baseline Gate; H1–H9 evidence; durable Core, isolated effects, constant acceptance bar |
| VH2 — Team studio | multiple missions/projects, executive portfolio, richer workspaces | reliable WIP/scheduling, typed memory lifecycle, registry trust, identity, accessible UX |
| VH3 — Connected organization | opt-in collaboration, remote workers, organization policy, shared knowledge | sync protocol, remote capability enforcement, data residency, multi-principal authority, accepted local evidence |
| VH4 — Ecosystem | signed marketplace, industry skill packs, partner integrations | robust package trust/revocation, compatibility, evaluation, support, and demonstrated demand |
| VH5 — Engineering network | federated/managed control planes and expert capability marketplaces | tenant isolation, audit portability, consent, economic/governance model, strict interoperability |

## Future product possibilities

- **Portfolio intelligence:** compare outcome equivalence, risk, throughput, cost, evidence, and architecture health across projects without surveilling people.
- **Architecture observatory:** compare approved architecture and specifications with dependencies, runtime topology, diagnostics, and accepted change impact.
- **Skill learning network:** promote locally measured skill improvements through evaluation and approval; share curated procedures without leaking project data.
- **Policy as product:** configurable autonomy envelopes, compliance packs, audit exports, and sector controls over the same Core semantics.
- **Remote capacity:** remote worker fleets and specialist services under the same task/effect/evidence contract as local workers.
- **Simulation and forecasting:** explore mission-plan, cost, risk, and staffing scenarios while labelling uncertainty and never presenting a simulation as execution evidence.
- **Human expertise marketplace:** assign trusted human reviewers/approvers through governed decisions, never by bypassing authority.

## Guardrails for growth

Future scope must preserve:

- policy → approved specification → Outcome Contract → delivery plan → phase plan → task packet → tool action authority;
- brains reason, Hermes supervises bounded work, builders/subagents produce artifacts, Core governs, tools enforce, evidence verifies, and humans approve where required;
- accepted quality does not vary by model profile;
- knowledge and memory retain provenance, classification, freshness, contradiction, expiry, and supersession;
- extensions remain capability-limited and cannot become hook/plugin escape paths;
- source code remains owned by Git and accepted changes remain reviewable proposals;
- local use remains valuable without cloud collaboration.

WePLD should not pursue a general consumer chat assistant, uncontrolled agent swarm, universal agent/language/model standard, autonomous production deployment, total codebase surveillance, cross-customer learning, open marketplace, remote fleet, or cloud-first control plane before the local governed system proves safety, consent, provenance, outcome value, and economic value.

## Research agenda

Before VH3+, evaluate secure cross-device ledger/artifact replication, remote envelope attestation, confidential model execution, policy interoperability, portable evaluation bundles, human-in-the-loop decision ergonomics, memory freshness/causal inference, data residency, and legal/compliance implications. Research runs against isolated fixtures and explicit privacy/security review.

Before VH4+, demonstrate that signed skills/hooks/packs can be evaluated, revoked, migrated, and audited without changing Core authority. Popularity, ratings, or provider marketing can never override policy or evidence.

## Measuring the vision

The long-term measure is not agents, prompts, models, tokens, or lines changed. It is whether users deliver better engineering outcomes with less routine coordination, a stable acceptance bar across providers, stronger quality/security evidence, lower rework, understandable decisions, bounded human attention, portable procedures, and durable institutional learning.

Near-term claims must come from the controlled protocol in [34_Harness_Evaluation_Protocol.md](34_Harness_Evaluation_Protocol.md); long-term claims require cohort and operational evidence rather than extrapolation from fixtures.
