# 29 — Future Vision

## Long-horizon aspiration

WePLD evolves from a trusted local engineering studio into a composable operating system for engineering organizations. Its advantage should compound: every completed mission yields governed evidence, reusable skills, validated patterns, and decision history that makes the next mission more reliable—not simply a larger prompt history.

## Horizons

| Horizon | Capability | Architectural prerequisite |
| --- | --- | --- |
| H1 — Trusted local studio | one executive, one project, governed autonomous loops | durable Core, worktree isolation, evidence gates, replaceable providers |
| H2 — Team studio | multiple missions/projects, executive portfolio, richer workspaces | reliable scheduler, knowledge lifecycle, registry trust, accessible UX |
| H3 — Connected organization | opt-in collaboration, remote workers, organization policy, shared knowledge | identity, sync protocol, remote capability enforcement, data residency |
| H4 — Ecosystem | signed marketplace, industry skill packs, partner integrations | robust package trust/revocation, compatibility, evaluation, support model |
| H5 — Engineering network | federated/managed control planes and expert capability marketplaces | tenant isolation, audit portability, economic/governance model, strict interoperability |

## Future product possibilities

- **Portfolio intelligence:** compare mission outcomes, risk, throughput, cost, and architecture health across projects with evidence, not surveillance.
- **Architecture observatory:** continuously compare declared architecture, runtime topology, dependency health, and change impact.
- **Skill learning network:** promote locally proven improvements through evaluation and approval; organizations can share curated skills without leaking project data.
- **Policy-as-product:** configurable autonomy envelopes, compliance packs, audit exports, and sector-specific controls that retain the same core semantics.
- **Remote capacity:** remote worker fleets and specialist services under the same lease/capability/evidence contract as local workers.
- **Simulation and forecasting:** run mission-plan, cost, risk, and staffing scenarios using historical evidence while labeling uncertainty clearly.
- **Human expertise marketplace:** assign trusted human reviewers/approvers through Messenger/decision workflows, never by bypassing the control plane.

## Guardrails for future growth

Future scope must not violate the foundational separations: brains do not execute; workers do not own mission state or user communication; policy mediates effects; evidence gates completion; knowledge has provenance/classification; extensions remain capability-limited; source code remains owned by Git; and local use remains valuable even when cloud collaboration exists.

WePLD should not pursue a universal agent standard, autonomous production deployment, total codebase surveillance, cross-customer learning, or a public marketplace before it can prove safety, consent, provenance, and economic value. Scale is not permission to dilute governance.

## Research agenda

Before H3+ investment, evaluate: secure cross-device event/blob replication; confidential model execution and attestation; portable sandbox standards; policy language/interoperability; agent evaluation benchmarks; human-in-the-loop decision ergonomics; knowledge freshness/causal inference; and legal/compliance implications of autonomous code changes. Conduct research with isolated fixtures and explicit privacy review.

## Measuring the vision

The long-term measure is not number of agents, prompts, or lines changed. It is whether organizations deliver better engineering outcomes with less routine coordination, stronger quality/security evidence, lower rework, understandable decisions, portable capabilities, and durable institutional knowledge.
