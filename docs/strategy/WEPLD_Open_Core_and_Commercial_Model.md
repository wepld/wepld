# WePLD Open Core and Commercial Model

**Standing:** planning only; a recommendation for founder decision, not a
commitment.

## Principle

The open project must remain independently useful and credible. The boundary
is drawn so that **trust-critical substance is open** (anyone can verify the
governance actually governs, and anyone can leave with their truth), while
**coordination, scale, and certification services are commercial**. The
boundary is explicitly not designed to maximize lock-in — the Sovereignty and
Exit Pack is an open-source obligation precisely so the commercial offer must
win on merit.

## Open-source foundation (recommended)

- AGILLE methodology (publishable as an independent method);
- artifact and evidence schemas (the exit guarantee — open from Stage 2);
- Intelligence Provider Protocol + Engineering Worker Protocol;
- conformance tests and fixtures;
- local Core (single-project authority, ledger, firewall);
- basic Hermes worker;
- basic local Studio (Mission Control essentials);
- policy schemas;
- Skill format;
- Committee format (documents 36–37 contracts);
- Sovereignty and Exit Pack (open-format export of events, artifacts,
  evidence, decisions, memory, skills; provider/agent/skill/evidence
  portability).

## Commercial services (recommended)

- polished collaborative Studio;
- hosted coordination (multi-user, multi-project);
- managed provider routing and metering;
- enterprise policy management;
- audit retention at compliance depth;
- organization learning (org memory, org Skills);
- managed private SkillHouse;
- certified global SkillHouse and certified Skill distribution;
- enterprise/sovereign deployment tooling and support;
- managed execution capacity;
- support and SLAs;
- compliance **evidence mapping** (machinery and mappings — never a claim of
  automatic legal compliance).

## Entitlement and metering

WePLD product entitlement (subscription via a Stripe-class provider
abstraction) is metered separately from provider usage (tokens/compute passed
through or resold with margin). Engineering truth is never hostage: export
works at every tier.

## Moat

The moat is not a model and not a prompt library. It is (1) the governed
delivery substrate — ledgered truth, evidence-bound completion, capability
leasing; (2) the compounding, judged project intelligence (DNA, Truth Graph,
memory, certified Skills) that stays with the customer but works best inside
the method; (3) the certification and trust infrastructure (Arena, Trust
Registry, SkillHouse certification) whose value grows with ecosystem
participation; and (4) protocol neutrality itself — the credible promise that
providers, editors, and agents are replaceable makes WePLD the safe place to
standardize.

## Founder decisions still required

1. Open-source license family (permissive vs copyleft vs source-available for
   which components).
2. Hosted-first vs local-first commercial sequencing.
3. Consulting independence policy: is a second model family required for
   independent review at V0, or is structural separation sufficient initially?
4. Committee investment timing given doc-37 evaluation cost.
5. Studio technology stack and desktop-vs-web posture.
6. AGILLE as an independently branded methodology: publish when?
7. Initial wedge: prosumer/solo-senior vs early enterprise design partners.
8. SkillHouse certification economics (who pays for evaluation).
9. Provider partnership posture vs strict neutrality marketing.
10. Telemetry default (recommendation: off by default at every tier) and the
    pricing metering unit (missions vs seats vs usage).
